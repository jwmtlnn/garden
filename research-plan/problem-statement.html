Problem Statement

Purpose of the Study

This research aims to understand how the applied research, development and innovation activities in Finnish Universities of Applied Sciences work socio-technically, and to examine their role in their respective UAS and more broadly in different National Innovation Systems. The research adopts a constructivist views of technical innovation to explain the inner workings of these RDI organizations in relation to relevant innovation systems. Main theoretical backgrounds and lenses are those of Actor-Network Theory (ANT) and systems of innovation (i.e. National System of Innovation, NIS). The deliverable objective of this project is to create an ANT and NIS inspired rich socio-technical ethnographic description about these kinds of research/RDI groups in relation to and their roles and activities in relevant systems of innovation. The aim is to tell a dynamic story of how innovation is actively forged through the alignment of diverse human and material elements, and how these efforts contribute to the vitality of the regional innovation system in a "learning economy".

Scope and Demarcation

The subjects of the study are the Lapland University of Applied Sciences, it's RDI groups, stakeholders and the innovation systems in their operational field. The topic is their RDI activities and the context is regional-national.

Research Questions

  • What are the actor-networks of the RDI groups?
    • How do RDI groups enact the process of translation to define problems, successes and failures, enroll key actors, and build stable innovation networks?
    • Who and what are the decisive actors in these networks, and how are their diverse interests and controversies aligned?
    • What inscription devices are used to stabilize the network and make innovations durable and mobile within the region?
  • How do they relate to the National Innovation System?
    • In what ways do the network-building activities of RDI groups contribute to the knowledge flows and learning processes characteristic of a regional "learning economy"?
    • How does the institutional position of UASs as practice-oriented, "semi-academic" actors shape their function as intermediaries or boundary-spanners within the regional innovation system?
    • What specific roles do these RDI groups perform that influence the overall structure and dynamism of their regional innovation system?

Significance of the Study

The impacts pursued with this study are to evoke discussion in both theoretical and societal dimensions. General theoretical discussion this research contributes is how the actor-network theory portrays a system of innovation. A more particular discussion is the relationship between network-building (ANT) and learning economy (NIS) in a regional system with semi-academic actors. Societal discussion it partakes in is innovation policy and the role of UAS’s in innovation systems in Finland, particularly in regional settings. To answer these objectives, the following auxiliary research questions are asked:

  • What are the implications for theory?
    • How can an ANT lens offer a more dynamic and process-oriented account of how a System of Innovation is actively constructed and maintained?
    • What is the precise relationship between the micro-dynamics of network-building (ANT) and the emergent properties of the regional "learning economy" (NIS) as seen through the activities of UAS RDI groups?
  • What are the practical implications?
    • Based on this socio-technical analysis, what are the key leverage points and barriers for Finnish innovation policy aiming to enhance the role of UASs in fostering regional innovation?
    • How might a deeper understanding of the granular socio-technical realities of RDI work inform the strategic management, evaluation, and funding of RDI within the UAS sector itself?
